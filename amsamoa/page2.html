Hiking in Samoa: A Traveler’s Guide

Samoa offers stunning hiking trails through lush rainforests, volcanic peaks, and coastal cliffs. Whether you seek adventure or scenic views, these trails showcase the island’s natural beauty.

Top Trails

  • Mt. Vaea (Upolu) – A short climb to Robert Louis Stevenson’s tomb with scenic Apia views.
  • O Le Pupu-Pu'e Park – Rainforest trails, waterfalls, and lava cliffs.
  • Lake Lanoto'o – A jungle trek to a crater lake with goldfish.
  • Mt. Silisili (Savai’i) – A challenging multi-day hike to Samoa’s highest peak.
  • Alofaaga Blowholes – A coastal walk leading to powerful ocean geysers.

Hiking Tips

  • Wear sturdy shoes for rugged trails.
  • Carry water, as some hikes are remote.
  • Respect village customs and ask for permission if needed.
  • Be mindful of weather conditions.
  • Hire a guide for difficult treks like Mt. Silisili.

Chasing Waterfalls in Samoa

Discover Samoa’s stunning waterfalls, surrounded by lush forests and offering perfect spots for swimming and sightseeing.

Top Waterfalls to Visit

  • Afu Aau Falls (Savai'i) – A crystal-clear pool, perfect for swimming.
  • Togitogiga Falls (Upolu) – Once a warrior’s bathing site, now a great picnic spot.
  • Papapapaitai Falls (Upolu) – One of Samoa’s tallest waterfalls, best viewed from the Cross-Island Road.
  • Sopoaga Falls (Upolu) – A scenic waterfall surrounded by lush gardens.
  • Fuipisia Falls (Upolu) – A hidden gem with a breathtaking top-down view.

Travel Tips

  • Wear proper footwear – trails can be slippery.
  • Pack swimwear – many waterfalls have natural pools.
  • Respect local customs – some waterfalls require small entry fees.
  • Check weather conditions – rain may impact accessibility.
  • Leave no trace – help preserve Samoa’s natural beauty.
